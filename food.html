Kazakh Cuisine

Discover the rich flavors of Kazakhstan's traditional dishes.

Famous Delicacies

Beshbarmak

Known as the national dish of Kazakhstan, Beshbarmak (translates to "five fingers") consists of boiled horse or lamb meat served over flat noodles, accompanied by an onion sauce and a rich broth called shorpa.

Kazy

Kazy is a traditional horse meat sausage seasoned with garlic and pepper, then smoked and dried. It's a delicacy often served during festive occasions and symbolizes Kazakh hospitality.

Baursak

Baursak are fluffy, deep-fried dough balls, often compared to doughnuts. They are commonly served as a snack or dessert, especially during celebrations and holidays.

Kuyrdak

Kuyrdak is a traditional dish made from roasted sheep or cow offal, such as liver, heart, and kidneys, saut√©ed with onions and spices. It's a hearty meal that showcases the resourcefulness of Kazakh cuisine.

Shubat

Shubat is fermented camel's milk, a traditional Kazakh beverage known for its refreshing taste and probiotic qualities. It's especially popular in the summer months.

Kumys

Kumys is fermented mare's milk, a traditional drink believed to have health benefits. It has a slightly sour taste and a low alcohol content, making it a unique cultural experience for visitors.
